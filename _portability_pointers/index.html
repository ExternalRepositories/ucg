Some hints, tips, and things I've discovered in my efforts to write portable code in the 21st century.

The First Rule of Portability is: Nothing is Portable

While it sounds like hyperbole (I mean, after all, this *is* the 21st century! ...Right?), the fact of the matter is, virtually nothing is portable. Ok, ok, maybe not _nothing_, but if you need to use it in your program, or to build your program, or to test your program, chances are it ain't portable. And I'm just talking POSIX-oid platforms here.
